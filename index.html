TOP 5Most common mistakes — from 10 WA1 papers▼
MistakeFix
Wrong passive form ("was borned")"Born" is already past participle. Never add -ed.
Double comparative ("more older")Use -er OR "more" — never both together.
"enjoy to swim" / "suggest to go"enjoy/suggest/avoid/consider → always + -ing
"less students" (countable)fewer = countable nouns. less = uncountable.
"since two hours"since = specific point. for = duration.
TENSESQuick tense decision guide▼
Signal wordsTense to useExample
every day / usually / alwaysPresent SimpleShe walks to school every day.
now / at this moment / right nowPresent ContinuousThey are playing football now.
since / for (still ongoing)Present Perfect / Perf. Cont.He has lived here since 2015.
when [past] + action in progressPast ContinuousShe was cooking when I called.
before [past time] / by [past time]Past PerfectHe had left by 8 p.m.
was/were + past participlePassive (past)The cake was baked yesterday.
will / next / tomorrowFuture SimpleWe will visit them next week.
ARTICLESa / an / the / no article — decision rules▼
RuleExample
Vowel SOUND → anan elephant, an honest man, an MBA, a European
Unique things (sun, moon, earth)The sun rises. The Nile is long.
Superlatives always take "the"the best, the tallest, the most clever
Musical instruments → theplay the guitar, the piano, the violin
Names of mountains → no articleMount Everest, Mount Fuji (not "the Mount")
Oceans, rivers, seas → thethe Pacific, the Nile, the Amazon
